Data Exchange in Meteorology

The backbone of modern meteorology lies in seamless data exchange across global networks. Real-time satellite imagery, radiosonde profiles, and radar composites flow through dedicated telecom infrastructures, standardized by WMO protocols like GTS (Global Telecommunication System).

Time synchronization is paramount: solar clocks govern civil aviation while sidereal time governs celestial observations. METAR codes encapsulate hourly airport conditions in compact aviation-speak, parsed instantly by forecasting algorithms.

Solar to Sidereal Time Converter

Sidereal time lags solar time by approximately 3 minutes 56 seconds per day due to Earth's orbital motion. Astronomers and satellite operators use this correction for precise orbital predictions and stellar observations.

Input solar time (HH:MM:SS UTC) to compute corresponding sidereal time, essential for G-rib somesonde launches and radio occultation missions.
